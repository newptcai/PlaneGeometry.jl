The Theorem

PlaneGeometry.Theorems.Centroid — Module

The centroid of a triangle is the point of intersection of its medians (the lines joining each vertex with the midpoint of the opposite side). The Centroids Exists Theorem states that the three medians indeed intersect at single point.

Finding the centroid and medians

PlaneGeometry.Theorems.Centroid.centroid — Function
centroid(📐️)

Find the centroid and the three medians of 📐️.

source
function centroid(tri)
    medians = map(i->median(circshift(vertices(tri), i)...), 0:2)
    cpt = concurrent(medians)
    cpt, medians 
end

Proof

function centroid_proof()
    @vars by cx positive=true;
    @vars cy;

    A = Point(0, 0); B = Point(0, by); C = Point(cx, cy);
    tri = Triangle(A, B, C)

    cpt, medians = centroid(tri)
    cpt != nothing
end
does_thmhold(centroid_proof())
Theorem holds! 😀️
